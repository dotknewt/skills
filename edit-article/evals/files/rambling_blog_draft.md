# why we switched our standup to async

so ok this is kind of a long story but basically we used to do standup every morning at 9:30 and honestly half the team was late or half asleep and it just felt like a waste of everyone's time, like you'd sit there for 20 minutes listening to updates that didn't affect you at all, and I remember one time Priya was mid-sentence about a deploy issue and someone's kid walked into frame and that was basically the whole meeting, anyway.

so eventually we just started writing our updates in a slack channel instead, whenever we felt like it, morning or afternoon whatever, and it turns out that actually worked way better for us because people could read the updates when they had context-switched into "team mode" instead of getting pulled out of deep work at a random time, and also nobody had to be on camera which some people really appreciated, especially the folks on the west coast who were basically still waking up.

there were some downsides though, like it's harder to catch blockers in real time because someone might post their update at 11am and you don't see it until 2pm, so we added a rule where if you're blocked you ping the channel directly instead of waiting for standup, which mostly fixed that.

## some things we learned

- async doesn't mean "never talk," it means "don't force everyone into the same 15 minutes"
- write updates like someone skimming will read them, not like a diary entry
- keep a weekly sync as a backstop for anything that needs a real conversation

anyway that's basically it, we've been doing this for like eight months now and nobody wants to go back, though we did have one new hire ask "wait so when IS standup" in their first week which was funny.
