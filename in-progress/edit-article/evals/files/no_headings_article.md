We started the pilot program in March with six warehouses and a shared spreadsheet for tracking defect rates, which was clearly not going to scale but got us moving fast while the real tooling was still being built.

By June we had the dashboard live and could finally see defect rates broken down by shift and by line, which immediately surfaced a pattern nobody had noticed by hand: the night shift on line 3 had roughly double the defect rate of every other shift and line combination, and it turned out to be a lighting issue that made a specific seam hard to inspect visually after dark.

Fixing the lighting dropped line 3's night shift defect rate to roughly in line with the rest of the plant within two weeks, and that single fix effectively paid for the whole pilot program in avoided rework.

We're now rolling the dashboard out to the other four plants, starting with the two that have the highest current defect rates, and we expect similar quick wins there even though we don't know yet what the specific root causes will turn out to be.

The main open question is whether the lighting fix is a one-off or whether it points to a broader pattern of "conditions that make inspection harder than the process assumes," which we're planning to investigate as part of the wider rollout.
