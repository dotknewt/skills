# scratch notes - status meeting thing

[voice memo, 7:42am, walking to coffee]
ok so. we killed the friday status meeting three months ago and nobody has
asked for it back. that's the whole thing right there actually.

- everyone always came in with the update already written in slack the night before anyway
- the meeting was just... reading the slack post out loud? for 25 min?
- one time we canceled it bc of a holiday and literally nothing broke

quote from Priya in retro: "I stopped taking notes in that meeting like a year
before we killed it, i just doom-scrolled"

thing i keep coming back to: status meetings are a tax on the people who
already did the update-writing work, paid to the people who didn't.

- what replaced it: an async thread, people post when they're actually
  blocked, not on a schedule
- blocked-ness is rare! most weeks nobody posts anything
- the loud silence freaked people out at first. by week 3 it felt normal

[7:51am]
counter-argument i should address: new hires. the meeting was their only
regular signal of "what is everyone working on." async thread doesn't
replace that for someone who doesn't know who anyone is yet.

- possible fix: pair new hires with someone for the first 2 weeks instead of
  making everyone sit through a meeting to solve a new-hire problem

random aside, probably cut: this is basically the same argument as open-plan
offices vs quiet ones — optimizing for the median case makes edge cases worse

other team (data-eng) tried killing their standup too and it didn't work,
worth asking why before I claim this generalizes
